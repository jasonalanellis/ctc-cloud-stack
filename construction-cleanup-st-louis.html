The 3-Phase Construction Cleanup Protocol in St. Louis

Reputable construction cleanup companies serving Chesterfield, Clayton, Ladue, and St. Charles County follow a three-phase protocol that aligns with general contractor handoff requirements.

Phase 1 – Rough Clean (During Construction)

  • Remove large debris: drywall scraps, packaging, cut materials
  • Broom sweep all surfaces
  • Wipe down window sills and ledges
  • Remove stickers from windows and fixtures

Phase 2 – Final Clean (Pre-CO Inspection)

  • HEPA H13 vacuum all surfaces including walls, ceilings, HVAC vents
  • Clean all windows inside and out (streak-free)
  • Wipe all surfaces with appropriate cleaners (no residue)
  • Clean all fixtures, outlets, switch plates
  • Mop hard floors with pH-neutral solution
  • Clean all bathrooms: grout, fixtures, chrome
  • Provide signed phase completion checklist for GC records

Phase 3 – Touch-Up Clean (Post-Move-In)

  • Address areas disturbed during punch-list work
  • Final wipe-down of surfaces touched during move-in
  • Typically 1–2 hour engagement

OSHA Silica Compliance for Construction Cleanup in St. Louis

The 2016 OSHA silica rule (29 CFR 1926.1153) requires construction cleanup crews to limit worker exposure to respirable crystalline silica. For cleanup work, this means crews must use HEPA-filtered vacuum systems rather than standard vacuums. A construction cleanup company in St. Louis that uses standard janitorial vacuums may actually spread silica particulate rather than remove it — and exposes the GC to potential liability.

Before hiring any construction cleanup company in St. Louis, ask for their written silica exposure control plan. A legitimate provider will have one on file.
