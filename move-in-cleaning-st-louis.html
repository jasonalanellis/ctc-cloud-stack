Common Pitfalls in DIY Move-In Cleaning

Many homeowners underestimate the complexity of a true deep clean, leading to several common mistakes:

1. Surface-Level Cleaning: Merely wiping down visible dirt leaves behind unseen bacteria, allergens, and grime in grout, appliance crevices, and high-touch areas. 2. Using Incorrect Products: Applying abrasive cleaners to delicate surfaces or using bleach on certain materials can cause permanent damage, costly to repair. 3. Cross-Contamination: Using the same cloth for the toilet and kitchen counter is a direct route to spreading pathogens. A professional service adheres to strict protocols to prevent this. 4. Neglecting Air Quality: Without proper equipment like HEPA vacuums, dust and allergens are simply stirred up, not removed, leading to poor indoor air quality. 5. Overlooking High-Touch Points: Light switches, doorknobs, cabinet pulls, and remote controls are often teeming with germs but frequently missed in basic cleaning.
