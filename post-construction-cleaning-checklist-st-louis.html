New construction and renovation projects generate up to 40 pounds of fine particulate dust per room—silica from drywall compound, cellulose from wood cutting, and mineral fibers from insulation. Standard vacuum cleaners recirculate particles as small as 0.1 microns back into the air. This checklist documents the professional 3-phase approach used by St. Louis post construction cleaning specialists to achieve move-in or occupancy-ready conditions.

Why Checklist Order Matters

Post construction cleaning fails when crews skip phases or perform steps out of sequence. The most common error: cleaning floors before all overhead work is complete, requiring floors to be re-cleaned. The correct sequence always flows top-down, rough-to-fine, room-by-room.

Critical: Never mop hard floors before vacuuming drywall dust. Drywall compound activates with water and creates a paste that bonds to flooring and grout lines. HEPA vacuum first, always.

Phase 1 — Rough Clean (Day 1)

Rough clean removes bulk debris and prepares surfaces for detail cleaning. This phase typically occurs immediately after construction wrap-up, before interior finishes are inspected.

Phase 1 Checklist

  • Remove all construction debris, scrap lumber, packaging material
  • Sweep ceilings, ceiling fans, light fixtures (top-down)
  • Vacuum all wall surfaces with brush attachment
  • Wipe window frames, sills, and tracks with dry microfiber
  • Remove construction stickers and labels from windows and fixtures
  • Sweep all floors — including inside cabinets and closets
  • HEPA vacuum floors (do not mop yet)
  • Wipe exterior of all cabinets and drawer fronts
  • Clean inside cabinet interiors — wipe shelf surfaces
  • Remove protective film from appliances, countertops, fixtures
  • Collect all trash and remove from property

Phase 2 — Final Clean (Day 2)

Final clean is the detail pass that achieves move-in ready conditions. HEPA H13 filtration is non-negotiable for this phase — standard shop vacs spread fine silica dust rather than capturing it.

Kitchen & Bathrooms

  • HEPA vacuum all horizontal surfaces, ledges, shelving
  • Clean range hood interior and filter
  • Deep-scrub all countertops — remove grout haze from tile
  • Clean interior and exterior of all appliances (oven, dishwasher, refrigerator)
  • Descale and polish all faucets and hardware
  • Deep-scrub toilets, inside tank if accessible
  • Clean shower/tub — remove construction adhesive residue
  • Scrub grout lines (bathrooms and kitchen backsplash)
  • Polish mirrors — streak-free
  • Wipe all light switches, outlet covers, door handles

Living Areas, Bedrooms, Hallways

  • HEPA vacuum all baseboards and crown molding
  • Wipe all window glass — interior and exterior accessible panes
  • Clean interior window tracks and sills
  • Wipe all doors, door frames, and hinges
  • HEPA vacuum carpets with multiple passes (change filters mid-room)
  • Hard floor treatment: HEPA vacuum → pH-neutral damp mop → buff dry
  • Wipe all light fixtures and replace any burnt bulbs (if provided)
  • Clean closet interiors — shelving, rods, floors
  • Wipe stair balusters and railings

HVAC & Mechanical

  • Replace HVAC air filters (construction dust saturates filters)
  • Vacuum all duct registers and return air vents
  • Wipe thermostat housing
  • Clean garage floors — sweep and damp mop
  • Wipe garage door panels and tracks

Phase 3 — Punch-List Inspection (Day 2 or Day 3)

The punch-list pass is a joint walk-through with the contractor (or property manager) to verify every item on the construction punch list has been addressed. In St. Louis renovation projects, this phase determines whether the GC can deliver final keys to the owner.

Punch-List Checklist

  • Walk each room with contractor punch list in hand
  • Verify no adhesive, caulk, or paint overspray on floors
  • Confirm windows operate and lock correctly
  • Verify all fixtures are clean and operational
  • Check for any missed paint drips on finished floors
  • Confirm HVAC filter replaced and system operational
  • Final photographic documentation (before occupancy)
  • GC or property manager sign-off

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using bleach on new grout — bleach degrades grout sealer and causes discoloration within months
  • Machine buffing hardwood before HEPA vacuum — embeds grit into wood finish
  • Skipping duct register cleaning — HVAC then distributes dust throughout the property for months
  • Pressure washing interior windows — water intrusion damages window framing and triggers warranty disputes
  • Using one filter for the entire job — HEPA filters clog fast on construction sites; change filters every 1,000 sq ft

Frequently Asked Questions

How long after construction can you begin post construction cleaning?

The rough clean phase can begin as soon as construction trades finish their work in a given area. For large St. Louis custom homes, cleaning often starts room-by-room while finish work continues in other areas. Final clean should not begin until all construction activity is complete — paint touch-ups, trim work, and fixture installation must precede the detail pass.

Do I need post construction cleaning for a small bathroom renovation?

Yes. Even a single-bathroom tile replacement generates silica-laden grout dust that requires HEPA vacuuming to safely remove. Standard household vacuums recirculate fine particles. For small projects in St. Louis homes, a half-day professional service covers rough clean and final clean in one visit.

What's the difference between post construction cleaning and move-in cleaning?

Post construction cleaning addresses construction-specific contamination: drywall compound, paint overspray, adhesive residue, and silica dust. Move-in cleaning assumes a clean baseline and focuses on sanitation and cosmetic cleaning. In practice, post construction cleaning precedes and includes move-in cleaning for new builds and full-home renovations.
