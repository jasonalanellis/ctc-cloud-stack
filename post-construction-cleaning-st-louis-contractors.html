Scheduling Post Construction Cleaning Around Your Build Timeline

In St. Louis, the typical post construction cleaning request comes 24–72 hours before key handoff. Vendors who specialize in contractor accounts maintain reserved capacity for these short-notice requests. Here's how to time your cleaning order:

Rough Clean Timing

Schedule rough clean as soon as the last subcontractor trade finishes rough-in work. For new construction, this typically follows drywall mud and texture — before paint. Rough clean removes bulk debris and prepares surfaces for final finishes. Many Clayton and Chesterfield custom builders schedule rough clean as a standard line item immediately after drywall inspection.

Final Clean Timing

Final clean should begin after paint is complete and dry (typically 24 hours for latex, 48–72 hours for oil-based), all trim and millwork is installed, all fixtures are set, and all punch-list items from the GC's own review are complete. Starting final clean while trades are still on site guarantees a redo.

Punch-List Inspection

Schedule the punch-list walk-through for the morning after final clean — this gives time for any touch-up items without delaying owner handoff. In St. Louis custom home markets (Ladue, Frontenac, Town & Country), owners expect a documented clean before final walk-through.
